SERPENS MUTABILIS

Scientific Name:

Serpens Mutabilis

Discovered by / at:

Emma Blosser, Feb, 2025

Family:

Anomalous in classification, Serpens Mutabilis exhibits traits found in deep-sea serpentine species but lacks definitive ties to any known biological lineage. Some speculate it could be an evolutionary offshoot of gelatinous abyssal lifeforms, but its unique movement and structural adaptability set it apart.

Habitat:

Located exclusively in the deepest regions of the Mariana Trench, Serpens Mutabilis thrives in complete darkness, far beyond the reach of human civilization. It has been observed at depths exceeding 10,000 meters, where pressures are extreme and sunlight does not penetrate. Its presence suggests an adaptation to one of the most inhospitable environments on Earth, though how it sustains itself remains unknown.

Appearance:

Serpens Mutabilis is an elongated, fluid-like entity with a shape reminiscent of both an eel and a ribbon, yet its movement is more complex than either. Its body is segmented yet seamless, flexing and twisting with unnatural smoothness.

Key Characteristics:

  • Gliding Locomotion
  • Silent Feeding
  • Anti-Human
  • Slinkey Movement
